dividers

Thematic section breaks. One element, no variants. Hairline weight, neutral colour, asymmetric vertical margin — more space above than below, so a divider reads as a section ending and a new section starting.

when not to use a divider

Avoid — purely decorative

Don't use <hr> as a visual flourish or for rhythm without semantic meaning. A purely decorative line should be a <div> with aria-hidden="true" and a border-top. Screen readers will announce every <hr> as a separator.

Avoid — between adjacent list items

Don't use <hr> to separate list items. That's a list concern — the foundations/lists stylesheet handles it via border-bottom on <li>.

Avoid — where whitespace would do

Don't use <hr> where a heading's own margins already create the break. A divider plus a heading is often one element too many — let the heading do the work.
